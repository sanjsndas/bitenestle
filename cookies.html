Cookies Policy

Last Updated: June 15, 2023

Introduction

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, as well as to provide information to the website owners. Cookies typically contain the name of the website they come from, how long they will remain on your device, and a unique identifier. They help websites recognize your device when you return, remember your preferences, and provide you with a personalized experience. Cookies cannot carry viruses or install malware on your device, and they don't store sensitive personal information like credit card details.

2. Purpose of This Policy

This Cookies Policy explains how BiteNestle uses cookies and similar tracking technologies when you visit our website (bitenestle.com) or use our services. We want to be transparent about the types of cookies we use, their purposes, and how you can control them. This policy supplements our Privacy Policy and should be read in conjunction with it. By understanding our use of cookies, you can make informed choices about your privacy settings. We regularly review our cookie usage and update this policy as needed to reflect changes in technology or regulations.

3. Your Consent

When you first visit our website, you will see a cookie consent banner that asks you to accept or decline our use of non-essential cookies. Your consent preferences will be stored and respected for future visits. You can change your cookie preferences at any time by clicking the "Cookie Settings" link in the footer of our website. Essential cookies that are necessary for the basic functionality of our website will be placed regardless of your consent. Your continued use of our website after being presented with the cookie banner constitutes your acceptance of our use of cookies as described in this policy.

Types of Cookies We Use

4. Strictly Necessary Cookies

These essential cookies are necessary for the website to function properly and cannot be switched off in our systems. They are usually only set in response to actions made by you such as logging in, filling out forms, or setting your privacy preferences. These cookies do not store any personally identifiable information. Without these cookies, certain services you request cannot be provided. Examples include cookies that remember items in your shopping cart, maintain your session security, or enable load balancing during peak traffic times. You can set your browser to block these cookies, but some parts of the site will not work as a result.

5. Performance Cookies

These cookies allow us to count visits and traffic sources so we can measure and improve the performance of our site. They help us know which pages are the most and least popular and see how visitors move around the site. All information these cookies collect is aggregated and therefore anonymous. If you do not allow these cookies, we will not know when you have visited our site and will not be able to monitor its performance. Examples include Google Analytics cookies which help us understand how users interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously.

6. Functional Cookies

These cookies enable the website to provide enhanced functionality and personalization. They may be set by us or by third-party providers whose services we have added to our pages. If you do not allow these cookies, some or all of these services may not function properly. These cookies remember choices you make (such as your username, language, or region) and provide enhanced, more personal features. They may also be used to provide services you have asked for such as watching a video or commenting on a blog. The information these cookies collect may be anonymized and they cannot track your browsing activity on other websites.

7. Targeting Cookies

These cookies may be set through our site by our advertising partners. They may be used by those companies to build a profile of your interests and show you relevant advertisements on other sites. They work by uniquely identifying your browser and internet device. If you do not allow these cookies, you will experience less targeted advertising. These cookies are also used to limit the number of times you see an advertisement and help measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns. They remember that you have visited a website and this information may be shared with other organizations such as advertisers.

8. Social Media Cookies

These cookies are set by social media services we have added to our site to enable you to share our content with your friends and networks. They are capable of tracking your browser across other sites and building up a profile of your interests. This may impact the content and messages you see on other websites you visit. If you do not allow these cookies, you may not be able to use or see these sharing tools. Examples include cookies from Facebook, Twitter, and Pinterest that are used when you share content using social media buttons or when you sign in using social media accounts.

9. First-Party vs Third-Party Cookies

First-party cookies are set by the website you are visiting and can only be read by that site. Third-party cookies are set by organizations other than the website owner, such as analytics companies, advertising networks, and social media platforms. We use both first-party cookies (to enable core site functionality) and third-party cookies (for analytics and advertising purposes). Third-party cookies may track your use of our website and other websites to build a profile of your interests. You can control third-party cookies through your browser settings or by using privacy tools specifically designed for this purpose.

10. Session vs Persistent Cookies

Session cookies are temporary cookies that remain on your device until you close your browser. They enable websites to link your actions during a browser session. Persistent cookies remain on your device for the period specified in the cookie (from a few minutes to several years). They are activated each time you visit the website that created that particular cookie. We use both types of cookies - session cookies for essential functions like maintaining your logged-in state, and persistent cookies for functions like remembering your preferences and analyzing long-term user behavior patterns.

Cookie Management

11. Browser Controls

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings preferences. You can set your browser to refuse all or some browser cookies, or to alert you when websites set or access cookies. If you disable or refuse cookies, please note that some parts of our website may become inaccessible or not function properly. The methods for managing cookies vary by browser, but are typically found in the "Options," "Tools," or "Preferences" menu. For more information about how to modify your browser settings to block or filter cookies, visit your browser's help documentation or sites like www.allaboutcookies.org.

12. Opt-Out Tools

For analytics cookies, you can opt out of Google Analytics by installing Google's opt-out browser add-on. For targeted advertising cookies, you can visit the Digital Advertising Alliance's opt-out page (optout.aboutads.info) or the Network Advertising Initiative's opt-out page (optout.networkadvertising.org). These industry-developed programs allow you to opt out of interest-based advertising from participating companies. Please note that opting out doesn't mean you won't see ads - it means the ads you see may be less relevant to your interests. Also, these opt-outs are device and browser specific, so you'll need to opt out on each device and browser you use.

13. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers incorporate a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that, when activated, signals to websites that you do not want to be tracked. Currently, our website does not respond to DNT browser signals because no industry standard for DNT has been adopted. We continue to monitor developments around DNT browser technology and will reassess our approach if a standard emerges. In the meantime, you can use the other cookie control methods described in this policy to manage tracking on our site. We honor the Global Privacy Control (GPC) signal in jurisdictions where it is legally required.

14. Mobile Device Controls

On mobile devices, you may have additional controls over cookies through your device operating system. Both iOS and Android provide settings that allow you to control how cookies and other tracking technologies are used by mobile applications and browsers. These controls are typically found in the "Privacy" or "Security" sections of your device settings. Some mobile devices offer "Limit Ad Tracking" or "Opt out of Ads Personalization" features that affect how cookies are used for advertising purposes. Please consult your device documentation for specific instructions on managing cookies on your mobile device.

15. Cookie Settings on Our Site

In addition to browser controls, we provide a cookie preference center where you can customize your cookie settings for our website. You can access this by clicking the "Cookie Settings" link in our cookie banner or in the footer of our website. The preference center allows you to enable or disable different categories of cookies (except strictly necessary cookies). Your preferences will be saved and applied to your current browsing session and future visits. If you clear your browser cookies, you will need to reset your preferences. We use a cookie to remember your preferences, so if you block all cookies, your preferences won't be saved.

Specific Technologies

16. Google Analytics

We use Google Analytics to collect information about how visitors use our website. The cookies collect information in an anonymous form, including the number of visitors, where visitors come from, and pages visited. We use this information to compile reports and help us improve the site. Google Analytics cookies do not store personal information about you. We have configured Google Analytics to anonymize IP addresses and to not share data with other Google services. You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on or by adjusting your cookie preferences in our cookie settings.

17. Facebook Pixel

The Facebook Pixel is a piece of code that helps us track conversions from Facebook ads, optimize ads, build targeted audiences for future ads, and remarket to people who have taken certain actions on our website. The Pixel uses cookies to track user behavior after they have clicked on a Facebook ad. This allows us to measure the effectiveness of our advertising and show relevant ads to users who have visited our site. The data collected remains anonymous and we cannot see the personal data of individual users. However, Facebook may connect this data with your Facebook account and use it for its own advertising purposes.

18. Hotjar

We use Hotjar to better understand our users' needs and to optimize our service and experience. Hotjar is a technology service that helps us better understand our users' experience (e.g., how much time they spend on which pages, which links they choose to click, what users do and don't like, etc.). Hotjar uses cookies and other technologies to collect data on our users' behavior and their devices. This includes device IP address (processed during your session and stored in a de-identified form), device screen size, browser information, geographic location (country only), and preferred language. Hotjar stores this information in a pseudonymized user profile.

19. Advertising Cookies

We work with third-party advertising partners who use cookies to serve ads on our behalf across the internet. These advertising networks may collect information about your visits to our website and other websites to provide you with targeted advertising based on your browsing interests. The information collected through this process does not enable us or them to identify your name, contact details, or other personally identifying details unless you choose to provide these. You can control advertising cookies through our cookie preference center or through industry opt-out programs like the Digital Advertising Alliance or Network Advertising Initiative.

20. Affiliate Marketing Cookies

We participate in affiliate marketing programs, which means we may earn commissions on purchases made through our links to retailer sites. These programs use cookies to track referrals from our site to the retailer. The information collected is used to credit our site for the referral and does not include personal information about you. Our participation in these programs does not influence our content, and we only recommend products we genuinely believe will benefit our users. You can control these cookies through your browser settings or our cookie preference center, though doing so may affect our ability to earn commissions that support our site.

Legal Compliance

21. GDPR Compliance

For users in the European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom, and Switzerland, we comply with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) requirements regarding cookies and tracking technologies. This includes obtaining explicit consent before placing non-essential cookies, providing clear information about cookie usage, and making it easy for users to withdraw consent. We classify cookies according to their purpose and obtain separate consent for different categories. Users can manage their cookie preferences at any time through our cookie settings. We also honor the Global Privacy Control (GPC) signal from browsers that support it.

22. CCPA Compliance

For California residents, we comply with the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) requirements regarding cookies and tracking technologies. The CCPA gives California residents the right to opt out of the "sale" of personal information, which may include certain types of cookie-based tracking for advertising purposes. We provide a "Do Not Sell My Personal Information" link in our website footer that allows California residents to opt out of such tracking. We also honor the Global Privacy Control (GPC) signal. California residents can request information about the categories of personal information collected through cookies and how this information is used.

23. ePrivacy Directive

We comply with the EU ePrivacy Directive (Cookie Law) which requires websites to obtain informed consent from visitors before storing or retrieving non-essential information on a user's device. Our cookie consent banner provides clear information about cookie usage and allows users to make granular choices about which types of cookies to accept. We maintain records of consent and provide easy mechanisms for withdrawing consent. The banner reappears if cookie preferences are reset or after a significant change to our cookie policy. We conduct regular audits of our cookie usage to ensure compliance with evolving regulations.

24. International Data Transfers

Some of our third-party cookie providers may process data outside your country of residence, including in the United States. When we transfer personal data collected through cookies across borders, we implement appropriate safeguards as required by applicable law. For transfers from the EEA, UK, or Switzerland to the U.S., we rely on standard contractual clauses or other approved transfer mechanisms where required. You can find more information about international data transfers in our Privacy Policy. Some cookie providers may participate in privacy frameworks like the EU-U.S. Privacy Shield (invalidated but some still follow its principles).

25. Children's Privacy

Our website is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 through cookies or other means. For children between 13-16 in jurisdictions where parental consent is required, we do not set non-essential cookies without verifiable parental consent. We encourage parents to monitor their children's online activities and to consider using parental control tools available from online services and software manufacturers that help provide a child-friendly online environment. These tools can prevent children from disclosing personal information without parental permission.

Data Retention

26. Cookie Duration

The duration for which a cookie remains on your device depends on whether it is a "persistent" or "session" cookie. Session cookies are temporary and expire when you close your browser. Persistent cookies remain on your device for the period specified in the cookie (from a few minutes to several years). We set cookie durations based on the purpose of the cookie, with longer durations for cookies that remember preferences and shorter durations for security and session cookies. You can delete cookies manually through your browser settings at any time. Some cookies may renew their duration if you revisit our site before they expire.

27. Log Data Retention

Data collected through cookies may be stored in server logs for a limited period necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this policy. Analytics data is typically aggregated and anonymized, with raw data deleted after processing. Server logs containing IP addresses are usually retained for 30-90 days for security and troubleshooting purposes before being deleted or anonymized. Some data may be retained longer when required by law or necessary to protect our legal rights. Advertising partners may retain cookie data according to their own retention policies, which you can typically find in their privacy policies.

28. User Preference Retention

Your cookie preferences are stored in a cookie named "cookie_consent" that lasts for one year. This allows us to remember your choices across browsing sessions. If you delete this cookie or use a different browser or device, you will need to reset your preferences. When the cookie expires, you will be prompted to renew your consent. We maintain records of consent for audit purposes, including the date and time of consent, the consent mechanism used, and the preferences selected. These records are kept for the period required by applicable laws (typically 3-5 years).

Security Measures

29. Cookie Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect cookies and the data they contain. This includes using secure, HTTP-only flags for authentication cookies to prevent access via client-side scripts, and secure flags to ensure cookies are only transmitted over HTTPS connections. We regularly review our cookie usage to identify and mitigate potential security risks. Session cookies are given limited lifespans and automatically expire after periods of inactivity. We implement same-site cookie attributes to protect against cross-site request forgery (CSRF) attacks. While we take reasonable security measures, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure.

30. Data Minimization

We practice data minimization in our use of cookies, collecting only what is necessary for the purposes described in this policy. Where possible, we use anonymized or pseudonymized data in cookies. We regularly audit our cookie usage to eliminate unnecessary cookies and reduce data collection. For analytics, we use IP anonymization and configure tools to collect only aggregated data. We avoid storing sensitive personal information in cookies, instead using them primarily for identifiers and preferences. Our cookie settings allow you to further minimize data collection by opting out of non-essential cookies.
