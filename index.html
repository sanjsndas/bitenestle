Cooking Tips & Tricks

Perfect Pasta Every Time

Use plenty of water (4-6 quarts per pound of pasta) and salt it well (about 2 tablespoons per pound). Stir occasionally to prevent sticking and test for doneness a minute before the package suggests.

Making Richer Soups

For deeper flavor, sauté your vegetables until they're slightly caramelized before adding liquid. A splash of vinegar or lemon juice at the end can brighten flavors.

Keeping Bread Fresh

Store bread at room temperature in a paper bag or bread box. For longer storage, freeze sliced bread and toast slices as needed.

Better Meat Browning

Pat meat dry before cooking and don't overcrowd the pan. This ensures proper browning which equals more flavor in your finished dish.
