Privacy Policy

Last Updated: June 15, 2023

Introduction

1. Our Commitment to Your Privacy

At BiteNestle, we are deeply committed to protecting your privacy and safeguarding your personal information. This Privacy Policy outlines how we collect, use, disclose, and protect the information you provide when using our website and services. We adhere to all applicable privacy laws and regulations, and we continuously review our practices to ensure they meet the highest standards of data protection. Your trust is important to us, and we want you to feel confident about how we handle your personal information.

2. Scope of This Policy

This Privacy Policy applies to all information collected through our website (bitenestle.com), mobile applications, and any related services, sales, marketing, or events. It covers both online and offline data collection, including information you provide when you register on our site, subscribe to our newsletter, participate in surveys, or interact with us in other ways. The policy does not apply to third-party websites or services that may be linked from our platform, which have their own privacy policies we encourage you to review.

3. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technologies, legal requirements, and other factors. When we make changes, we will revise the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to periodically review this policy to stay informed about how we are protecting your information. Your continued use of our services after any changes constitutes your acceptance of the revised Privacy Policy, except where further consent is required by law.

Information We Collect

4. Personal Information You Provide

We collect personal information that you voluntarily provide when you register for an account, subscribe to our newsletter, submit recipes or comments, participate in surveys, or contact us for support. This may include your name, email address, postal address, phone number, payment information, dietary preferences, and any other details you choose to share. We only collect personal information that is necessary for the purposes outlined in this policy, and we strive to minimize data collection to what is directly relevant and necessary.

5. Automatically Collected Information

When you visit our website, we automatically collect certain information about your device and browsing behavior through cookies and similar technologies. This includes your IP address, browser type, operating system, referring URLs, pages viewed, links clicked, and the dates/times of your visits. We use this information to analyze trends, administer the site, track user movements, and gather demographic information for aggregate use. This data helps us improve our services and enhance your user experience.

6. Information from Third Parties

We may receive information about you from third-party sources, such as social media platforms when you interact with us through these services, or from analytics providers and advertising partners. This may include social media profile information, preferences, and other data that you have made publicly available or that the third party has collected in accordance with their own policies. We combine this information with data we collect directly to provide more personalized services and content.

How We Use Your Information

7. To Provide and Improve Our Services

We use your information to operate, maintain, and improve our website and services, including processing your recipe submissions, managing your account, and providing customer support. This includes using data to troubleshoot problems, analyze site performance, and develop new features that better serve our community. We also use information to personalize your experience by showing you content and recommendations that are most relevant to your interests and preferences.

8. Communication Purposes

We may use your contact information to send you important notices about your account, service changes, or updates to our policies. With your consent, we may also send you newsletters, promotional materials, and other communications about our services. You can opt out of marketing communications at any time by following the unsubscribe instructions included in these emails or by contacting us directly. We do not share your contact information with third parties for their own marketing purposes without your explicit consent.

9. For Research and Analytics

We analyze user behavior and preferences to understand how our services are used, identify trends, and make data-driven decisions about improvements. This includes using aggregated and anonymized data for statistical purposes, market research, and to evaluate the effectiveness of our content and features. These analyses help us enhance user experience, develop new services, and ensure our platform remains relevant and valuable to our community.

Information Sharing and Disclosure

10. With Your Consent

We will share your personal information with third parties only when we have your explicit consent to do so. For example, if you choose to participate in a co-branded promotion or request information from one of our partners, we may share relevant information with that third party. We will always clearly disclose when information will be shared with third parties and give you the opportunity to opt in or out of such sharing arrangements.

11. Service Providers

We engage trusted third-party companies and individuals to perform services on our behalf, such as website hosting, payment processing, data analysis, email delivery, and customer service. These service providers have access to personal information needed to perform their functions but are contractually prohibited from using it for other purposes. We carefully vet all service providers and require them to maintain appropriate security measures to protect your information.

12. Legal Requirements

We may disclose your information if required to do so by law or in the good faith belief that such action is necessary to comply with legal obligations, protect and defend our rights or property, prevent fraud, protect the safety of our users or the public, or protect against legal liability. This includes responding to lawful requests from public authorities, such as subpoenas, court orders, or other legal processes.

Data Security

13. Security Measures

We implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures designed to protect your personal information from unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures include encryption, firewalls, secure socket layer (SSL) technology, regular security audits, and access controls. While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your personal information, no method of transmission over the Internet or method of electronic storage is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

14. Data Breach Procedures

In the event of a data breach that compromises your personal information, we have established procedures to promptly investigate the breach, assess the risk to individuals, and notify affected users and relevant authorities as required by applicable law. Our response may include steps to mitigate harm, such as resetting passwords, temporarily disabling accounts, or providing credit monitoring services where appropriate. We document all breaches, regardless of their size or impact, to improve our security practices.

15. Your Role in Security

While we work hard to protect your information, you also play an important role in maintaining security. We encourage you to use strong, unique passwords for your BiteNestle account and to avoid sharing your login credentials with others. Be cautious about the personal information you choose to share in public areas of our site, such as recipe comments or community forums, as this information may be viewed by other users and could be used to send you unsolicited messages.

Your Privacy Rights

16. Access and Correction

You have the right to access the personal information we hold about you and to request correction of any inaccurate, incomplete, or outdated information. You can review and update much of your account information by logging into your BiteNestle account. For other requests or if you need assistance, please contact us using the information provided at the end of this policy. We may need to verify your identity before processing certain requests to protect your privacy and security.

17. Data Portability

Where applicable by law, you may request a copy of your personal information in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format. This allows you to transfer your data to another service provider if desired. We will provide your information in a format that is readily usable and, where technically feasible, may transmit it directly to another organization at your request. There may be limitations to this right based on legal requirements or technical constraints.

18. Deletion Rights

You may request deletion of your personal information in certain circumstances, such as when the data is no longer necessary for the purposes collected, you withdraw consent, or the data was processed unlawfully. Note that we may need to retain certain information for recordkeeping purposes, to complete transactions, or to comply with legal obligations. After deletion, your information will be permanently erased from our active systems, though residual copies may remain in backups for a limited period.

Cookies and Tracking Technologies

19. Types of Cookies We Use

We use several types of cookies on our website: strictly necessary cookies that are essential for site functionality, performance cookies that help us understand how visitors use our site, functionality cookies that remember your preferences, and targeting/advertising cookies that deliver relevant ads. These cookies may be first-party (set by us) or third-party (set by our service providers). Most cookies are session cookies that expire when you close your browser, while persistent cookies remain for longer periods.

20. Cookie Management

You have control over cookies through your browser settings. Most web browsers automatically accept cookies but allow you to modify settings to decline cookies or alert you when cookies are being sent. If you disable cookies, some features of our website may not function properly. We also provide cookie preference tools on our site that allow you to opt out of certain non-essential cookies while maintaining essential functionality. Your choices are stored in a cookie, so you may need to reset preferences if you clear cookies.

21. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that lets you indicate you prefer not to be tracked across websites. Currently, we do not respond to DNT signals because no uniform technology standard for recognizing and implementing these signals has been established. We continue to monitor developments around DNT browser technology and will reassess our approach if a standard emerges. In the meantime, you can manage tracking through cookie settings and the privacy controls we provide.

Children's Privacy

22. Age Restrictions

Our website is not intended for children under the age of 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we become aware that we have inadvertently received personal information from a child under 13, we will delete such information from our records. Parents or guardians who believe their child has provided us with personal information without their consent should contact us immediately. We encourage parents to monitor their children's online activities and to use parental control tools.

23. Teen Privacy

For users between the ages of 13 and 18, we recommend obtaining parental consent before using our services or providing personal information. While we may collect limited information from teens to provide services, we avoid collecting sensitive data from this age group. We provide additional privacy protections for teen users where required by law, such as restrictions on marketing communications and limitations on data sharing with third parties.

24. Educational Resources

We support efforts to educate children and parents about online privacy and safety. Our website may include family-friendly recipes and cooking activities that children can enjoy with adult supervision, but we do not target these features directly to children or collect personal information from them. We encourage parents to explore online privacy resources and to discuss internet safety with their children regularly.

International Data Transfers

25. Cross-Border Data Flows

As an online service, your personal information may be processed in countries other than your country of residence, including the United States where our servers are located. These countries may have data protection laws that differ from those in your country. When we transfer personal information across borders, we implement appropriate safeguards as required by applicable law, such as standard contractual clauses or other legal mechanisms to ensure adequate protection for your data.

26. EU-US Data Transfers

For transfers of personal data from the European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom, or Switzerland to the United States, we rely on appropriate safeguards as permitted under applicable data protection laws. This may include the EU Standard Contractual Clauses or other approved transfer mechanisms. We regularly review the legal frameworks governing international data transfers and update our practices as needed to comply with evolving requirements.

27. Your Rights Under GDPR

If you are located in the EEA, UK, or Switzerland, you have certain rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), including the right to access, correct, delete, or restrict processing of your personal data, the right to data portability, and the right to object to processing. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. We will respond to all legitimate requests within one month, though this period may be extended for complex requests.

Third-Party Links and Services

28. External Websites

Our website may contain links to third-party websites, plugins, or services that are not operated by us. These links are provided for your convenience and do not signify our endorsement of these websites. We have no control over and assume no responsibility for the content, privacy policies, or practices of any third-party sites or services. We strongly advise you to review the privacy policy of every site you visit, especially before providing any personal information.

29. Social Media Features

Our website includes social media features such as Facebook "Like" buttons and Twitter "Tweet" buttons. These features may collect your IP address and which page you are visiting on our site, and may set a cookie to enable the feature to function properly. Your interactions with these features are governed by the privacy policy of the company providing them. We recommend being cautious about what personal information you share through social media platforms.

30. Embedded Content

We may display embedded content from third-party services such as YouTube videos, Google Maps, or recipe widgets from other platforms. Embedded content behaves as if you had visited the other website, and these websites may collect data about you, use cookies, embed additional third-party tracking, and monitor your interaction with the embedded content. We cannot control these third-party tracking technologies, so you should review the privacy policies of these services if you have concerns.

Advertising and Analytics

31. Online Advertising

We may work with third-party advertising partners to serve ads on our behalf across the internet and to help measure the effectiveness of our advertising campaigns. These partners may use cookies and similar technologies to collect information about your activities on our site and other websites to provide you with targeted advertising based on your interests. You can opt out of many interest-based ads through industry self-regulation programs like the Digital Advertising Alliance or Network Advertising Initiative.

32. Analytics Services

We use analytics services such as Google Analytics to help us understand how visitors use our website. These services use cookies and similar technologies to collect information such as how often users visit the site, what pages they visit, and what other sites they used prior to coming to our site. We use this information to improve our website and services. Analytics providers are contractually prohibited from using the data collected on our behalf except to help us conduct and improve our business.

33. Affiliate Marketing

We participate in affiliate marketing programs, which means we may earn commissions on purchases made through our links to retailer sites. These programs use cookies to track referrals from our site to the retailer. The information collected is used to credit our site for the referral and does not include personal information about you. Our participation in these programs does not influence our content, and we only recommend products we genuinely believe will benefit our users.

Data Retention

34. Retention Periods

We retain your personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, including for the purposes of satisfying any legal, accounting, or reporting requirements. The specific retention period depends on the nature of the information and the purpose for which it is processed. For example, we retain account information for as long as your account is active, and transaction data for the period required by tax laws. After the retention period expires, we securely delete or anonymize your information.

35. Backup Systems

We maintain backup systems as part of our business continuity and disaster recovery plans. These backups may retain copies of your information for a limited period even after you request deletion, until the backups are refreshed in accordance with our standard procedures. Information in backups is not actively processed and is only accessed if needed for system restoration. We have technical and organizational controls in place to protect the security of backup data.

36. Archival Purposes

In some cases, we may retain certain information in an archived form for historical or research purposes, or for statistical analysis. This archived data is typically anonymized or aggregated so it cannot be linked back to individuals. For example, we might retain anonymized recipe ratings and comments to analyze food trends over time, but would remove any identifiers linking this data to specific users if those users delete their accounts.

Contact Information

37. How to Contact Us

If you have any questions or concerns about this Privacy Policy or our privacy practices, please contact us using the information provided in the footer of this page. We have designated a privacy officer who oversees compliance with this policy and can address your inquiries or complaints. When contacting us about privacy matters, please provide sufficient details about your concern or request so we can properly investigate and respond. We aim to respond to all privacy-related inquiries within 30 days.

38. Dispute Resolution

If you have a complaint about our privacy practices, we encourage you to first contact us directly so we can attempt to resolve the issue. If we are unable to resolve your concern, you may have the right to lodge a complaint with the relevant data protection authority in your jurisdiction. For EU residents, this would typically be the supervisory authority in your country of residence. We will cooperate with appropriate regulatory authorities to resolve any complaints.

39. Policy Updates Notification

We will notify users of material changes to this Privacy Policy through prominent notices on our website or via email when appropriate. The notification will indicate the effective date of the updated policy and, when possible, provide a summary of key changes. We encourage you to periodically review this page for the latest information about our privacy practices. Your continued use of our services after changes become effective constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

40. Governing Law

This Privacy Policy and any disputes related to it will be governed by the laws of the State of California, without regard to its conflict of laws principles. Any legal actions or proceedings relating to this policy shall be brought exclusively in the state or federal courts located in San Francisco County, California, and you consent to the jurisdiction of such courts. If any provision of this policy is found to be invalid, the remaining provisions will remain in full force and effect.
